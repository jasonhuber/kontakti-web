Personal relationship

A CRM for one person who wants to stay on top of their contacts — not a sales team chasing a quota. Track people, log interactions, and remember who to follow up with.

Get started free View source on GitHub

Most CRMs are built for teams with quotas, managers, and 47 integrations you'll never use.

You just want to know who you talked to, what you discussed, and who needs a follow-up. Kontakti is for that.

No pipeline stages. No deal tracking. No monthly seat count. Just people, companies, and the history between you and them.

What's in it

People
Full contact profiles. Relationship strength from cold to close, last contacted date, follow-up reminders, linked company, tags, and a complete interaction history.
Companies
Group contacts by company. See every person at a company and every discussion you've had with them, together in one view.
Discussions
Log calls, meetings, emails, and messages. Add a summary, full notes, and link the people who were in the room.
Notes
Rich text notes on any contact or company. Export to your Obsidian vault as plain Markdown with wiki-links intact.
Activity feed
Everything that's happened across your network in reverse chronological order. Nothing falls through the cracks.
Contact import
Pull contacts from your phone's address book or Gmail. Deduplicates against what you already have, so no double entries.

Fully open source.

MIT licensed. All three codebases are public on GitHub. Fork it, self-host it, or build your own client on top of the API.

→ Web — Laravel 12 + React 18 → iOS — SwiftUI + SwiftData → Android — Kotlin + Jetpack Compose

Read the docs

Native mobile apps.

iOS and Android — both built natively, not wrapped in React Native. Offline-first: the app serves your cached contacts immediately and syncs when you're back online.

Import contacts from your phone's address book or Gmail directly into Kontakti. The same clean REST API backs the web, iOS, and Android apps — build your own client if you want.

Works with Obsidian.

Every note you write in Kontakti can export to your Obsidian vault as plain Markdown with wiki-links intact. The structured data lives in the database; the readable version lives in your vault.

Your data, in your format, in your vault — even if you stop using Kontakti tomorrow.
